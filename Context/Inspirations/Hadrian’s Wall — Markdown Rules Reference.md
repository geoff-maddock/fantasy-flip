1. Objective

You are a Roman general building and managing a milecastle on Hadrian’s Wall over 6 years.
Your goal is to score the most VP by developing your fort, growing your civilian infrastructure, improving your four attributes, and defending against Pict attacks, while minimizing Disdain. 

HadriansWallRulebook

Final score

At game end, score:

Renown

Piety

Valour

Discipline

Path card VP

minus Disdain penalty

Highest total wins. Ties are broken by:

least Disdain

most Path-card points

shared victory if still tied.

4. Game structure

The game lasts 6 Years. Each year has 3 broad phases:

Year setup

Simultaneous action phase

Pict attack / end of year

Unused workers and resources do not carry over unless a rule specifically says otherwise. Normally, unused pieces are returned to supply at year end. 

HadriansWallRulebook

5. Year setup

At the start of each year:

5.1 Reveal a Fate card

Reveal the top Fate card. Every player gains the workers/resources shown on the bottom half of that card. 

HadriansWallRulebook

5.2 Draw 2 player cards

Each player draws the top 2 cards from their player-card deck.

From those 2 cards:

choose 1 as your Path card

place the other as your Prospect card

Then reveal both. 

HadriansWallRulebook

Path card

Tuck it above your board in the current year slot

It scores at end game if you meet its condition

Prospect card

Place it face up to the right of your board for the year

Immediately gain the workers/resources shown in its lower-left section 

HadriansWallRulebook

5.3 Collect yearly production

Gain:

1 Resource per filled circle in Resource Production

1 Civilian per filled circle in Hotel

1 Builder per filled circle in Workshop

1 Attribute gain per filled circle in Road
Choose Renown, Piety, Valour, or Discipline for each Road circle gained that year.

6. General rules for actions

During the year, players act simultaneously and in any order, spending workers/resources to fill spaces on their sheets. 

HadriansWallRulebook

6.1 Filling boxes

Most actions tell you to:

pay a cost

fill the next legal box/space

gain anything printed in that space you just covered

6.2 Track order

Most tracks fill left to right unless the section says otherwise.
Examples:

Cohorts

Mining & Foresting

Wall Guard

Cippi

Wall

Fort

6.3 Year-number boxes

Some actions have a #-style year box.
When you use that action, write the current year number in the box instead of fully shading it. This indicates annual-use limits. 

HadriansWallRulebook

6.4 “Fill a box with X icon”

If an action or reward says to fill a box showing an icon, you fill the next legal box in that linked area.
Example: filling a cohort icon lets you fill the next box in one cohort.

7.4 Fort, Cippi, and Wall

These three tracks are linked by the fort’s development.

Costs

Cippi: pay 1 Resource

Wall: pay 1 Resource

Fort: pay 1 Builder or 1 Soldier 

HadriansWallRulebook

Dependency rule

You can only extend Cippi or Wall as far as the connected Fort allows.
The fort acts like the underlying prerequisite track. 

HadriansWallRulebook

Important clarification

The Cippi and Wall do not gate each other.
Each is checked against the Fort, not against the other. That matches both the rulebook wording and community clarification discussions.

So:

Wall can be ahead of Cippi

Cippi can be ahead of Wall

neither can exceed what the Fort has unlocked

Infrastructure

As you fill the Fort, you gain Infrastructure (orange flags).
Infrastructure is required for many left-sheet buildings. 

HadriansWallRulebook

When all players stop taking actions:

return unused workers/resources to supply

HadriansWallRulebook

9.2 Reading the attack

Only the cohort arrows/icons at the top of the revealed Fate cards matter here.
Count how many attacks hit:

left cohort

center cohort

right cohort 

HadriansWallRulebook

9.3 Defense check

For each cohort, compare:

number of attacks revealed
vs.

number of boxes filled in that cohort

You defend a cohort if filled boxes are equal to or greater than attacks there. 

HadriansWallRulebook

9.4 Valour / Disdain outcome

The grey flag for the current year shows the total outcome value.

If you fully defend, gain that much Valour

If attacks get through, gain 1 Disdain per attack that gets through, up to that year’s grey-flag amount

If some but not all get through, you get a mix:

Disdain for the attacks that penetrated

Valour for the remainder up to the grey-flag number

13. Path cards summary

Each Path card scores 1, 2, or 3 VP based on thresholds. 

HadriansWallRulebook

Architect — 1 / 2 / 3 Landmarks

Aristocrat — end with 4 / 2 / 0 Final Disdain

Engineer — 2 / 4 / 6 Large Buildings

Fighter — 1 / 2 / 3 completed Cohorts

Forager — 3 / 6 / 9 Resource Production boxes

Merchant — 4 / 6 / 8 Trade Goods in Market

Community rules discussions align with the appendix wording: a “wall section” means one of the three granary-bounded segments of the Wall track, not Cippi. Likewise Vanguard checks the Wall Guard track’s three sections.

16. Common rule clarifications
16.1 Does Wall depend on Cippi?

No.
Wall and Cippi each depend on Fort, not on each other.

16.2 Do duplicate Market numbers do anything?

Yes:

they still fill a market box

they still give any printed reward in that box

they just do not count as a new unique good for Renown

16.3 How much does filling a Temple cost?

1 worker for 1 box.

16.4 Can a neighbor use the Resource/Soldier immediately when I pay them?

Yes. The rulebook explicitly says a paid neighbor may immediately make use of that piece when buying Trade Goods or using Scout patterns from them. 

HadriansWallRulebook

16.5 Do unused workers/resources carry over?

Normally no. Return them at year end.
Exception: workers gained while resolving end-of-year Valour can carry into the next year, except after Year 6 when they are wasted. 

HadriansWallRulebook
